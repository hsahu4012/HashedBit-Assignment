Nrusinghanath Temple, Bargarh

Location: Sri Nrusinghanatha is a temple in Odisha, India, located at the foothills of Gandhamardhan hills near Paikmal, Bargarh.

Historical Foundation: The temple's foundation was laid by the King of Patna, Baijal Singh Dev, in the early 15th century CE.

Height: The temple stands at a height of only 45 feet.

Structure: The temple is divided into two main parts: the first part is the seat of Lord Nrusinghnath, and the second part is the Jagamohan (antechamber), which has three gates, each supported by four pillars.

Architectural Design: The Jagamohan, or the antechamber, is a distinct architectural feature of the temple, adding to its historical and cultural significance.

Shree Harishankar Temple, Balangir

1. Location: Sri Sri Harisankar Devasthana is situated on the slopes of the Gandhamardhan hills in the Bolangir District of Odisha, India.

2. Religious Significance: The temple is dedicated to two Hindu deities, Vishnu and Shiva, making it a significant site for devotees of both lords.

3. Natural Beauty: The temple is renowned for its scenic natural surroundings, including a stream that flows over a granite bed, offering visitors a sense of tranquility and peace.

4. Proximity to Nrusinghanath Temple: On the opposite side of the hills lies the temple of Nrusinghanath, creating a pair of significant religious sites in the region.

5. Historical Significance: The plateau between the two temples contains ancient Buddhist ruins, believed to be remnants of the ancient Parimalgiri University, highlighting the area's rich historical and cultural heritage.

Maa Samaleswari Temple, Sambalpur

1. Dedication: Samaleswari Temple is dedicated to the goddess Samaleswari, also known as Samalei Maa, meaning Mother Samaleswari, and is located in Sambalpur, Odisha, India.

2. Religious Significance: Shree Shree Samaleswari is the presiding deity of and is a significant religious force in the western part of Odisha and the Chhattisgarh state of India.

3. Worship and Titles: The mother goddess Samaleswari is worshipped from ancient as Jagatjanani, Adishakti, Mahalaxmi, and Mahasaraswati, and she is considered the mother of the universe.

4. Cultural Heritage: The temple is situated in a region with a rich cultural heritage, with Sambalpur region historically known as Hirakhanda. Ptolemy referred to it as Sambalaka.

5. Historical Accounts: According to French traveller Jean-Baptiste Tavernier and English historian Edward Gibbon, diamonds from Sambalpur were exported to Rome, indicating the region's historical significance.
